Before you build

Learn the essentials

Two short things: a cheat sheet for talking to Cowork, and three lessons to keep with you long after today.

What is Cowork?

Cowork is Claude working right on your computer, like a capable teammate at the next desk. It reads your files, drafts emails and documents, does research, builds simple tools, and organizes your work. You talk to it in plain English. There is nothing to code.

STARTA: how to ask so you get gold

The quality of what you get back depends on how you ask. Six simple moves.

S

Set the Role

Tell it who to act as: a coordinator, an assistant, an expert.

T

Tell it the Task

Be explicit about what you want done. Vague in, vague out.

A

Add Context

Give background, audience, and any constraints.

R

Reference Examples

Show what good looks like. Examples beat descriptions.

T

Tell it the Format

Say what you want: an email, a list, a length.

A

Ask for Questions

"Any questions before you begin?" This alone lifts quality.

Vague vs. clear

Same goal, very different results.

Vague

"Write an email to a customer."

Clear (STARTA)

"Act as our front-desk coordinator. Write a friendly email to a customer who asked about AC repair: thank them, ask the 3 things we need to schedule, and offer two time slots. Keep it under 120 words."

Lesson 1

Think before you ask

Do not outsource your thinking. Cowork can move fast, but speed without direction just makes a mess quickly.

Before any task, answer three questions: What do I want? Why does it matter? Who is it for? If you can answer those clearly, you'll get something useful. If you can't, neither can Cowork.

Lesson 2

A draft is not the final word

Cowork gives you a fast, strong starting point, not gospel. It can be confidently wrong, and it only knows what you give it.

Read what it produces. Fix the tone, check the facts, add what it missed. The polish and the truth are still your job, and that's exactly where your value shows.

Lesson 3

Agency and accountability

The most important one: don't forget what makes the work yours.

Every message and file that goes out under your name is your responsibility. Cowork is a powerful teammate, but the judgment, the relationships, and the final call stay human. Stay in the driver's seat.

Put it to work

Two tasks to try together

We'll do these live, then you'll run them yourself. Both solve real problems for Military Precision Home Solutions. Open Claude Cowork and follow along.

Task 1 · Communicate

Never miss a lead

A prospect called about AC and nobody called back for five days. That's a lost job. Here Cowork turns a rough inquiry into an instant, professional response, plus a template you can reuse forever.

  1. Open Claude Cowork on your desktop.
  2. Tell it about the inbound lead (or paste the message).
  3. Paste the prompt below and send it.
  4. Read the draft. Adjust the tone so it sounds like you.
  5. Ask Cowork to save the blank version as a reusable template.
Prompt
Act as the front-desk coordinator for Military Precision Home Solutions, a home services company. A customer just reached out because their air conditioning stopped working. Draft a fast, friendly callback in two versions, one email and one text, that: thanks them for reaching out, apologizes for any delay, asks the 3 things we need to schedule a visit, and offers two appointment windows this week. Keep it warm and under 120 words. Then give me a blank template I can reuse for any new lead. Ask me any questions before you start.
Task 2 · Build

Build a lead-capture page, live

Daron's top priority: a simple way to capture leads. Watch Cowork build a clean, branded intake page from a plain-English description. No code, and you'll see it appear in minutes.

  1. Open Claude Cowork.
  2. Paste the prompt below to describe the page.
  3. Watch it build the page and show you a preview.
  4. Ask for changes: different fields, colors, wording.
  5. Have it save the file so you can open it anytime.
Prompt
Build a simple, clean lead-capture page for Military Precision Home Solutions, a home services company. Include our name, a short friendly headline, and a form that collects: name, phone, email, service needed, and preferred time. Use a professional navy-and-orange look. Make it a single web page I can preview and save. Ask me any questions before you begin.
